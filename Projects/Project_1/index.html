【 INTRODUCTION 】

Infrastructure into which a building's program and architectural elements can be unpacked is proposed for public space and streetscapes in Chinatown. This system allows for the manipulation of architectural elements and materials such that they respond to social and cultural trends. The function of building fragments becomes altered by changing context from inside to outside, obfuscating the boundary between building and public space. The project redefines the pedestrian experience and creates an opportunity for city dwellers to use free time as a productive process of exchange and communication.

SLIDE

This first piece is cut from a cafe. The slide highlighted in yellow serves as a bar counter in the interior space and suggests a different application of that building part versus the surrounding space. While relocating to the public space, the fragment can choose to rotate in 90 degrees and function as a slide for children or skaters and a pavilion for a food market, for intense.

| FRAGMENT B |

SWIMMING POOL

The second condition reveals the program of a swimming pool inside a building. The visual effect produced by the water of the swimming pool becomes part of the spatial experience within a working space. The fragment contains a portion of the swimming pool and the office, which adds a new spatial quality and potential of using the office while outside the building.
